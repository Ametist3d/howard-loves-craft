CRITICAL SYNTHESIS RULES:
- Preserve the seed's central anomaly, social setup, and investigative premise.
- Use atoms as support material, not as replacement plots.
- Prefer coherence over novelty.
- You may combine elements from multiple atoms, but do NOT overwrite the seed with a stronger stock-horror template.
- Keep the early scenario close to the seed's ordinary surface situation.
- Do not introduce a cult, ritual, portal, ancient god, or final-banishing spell unless the seed itself clearly points there.
- If an atom represents an endgame mechanic, do not let it dominate the hook or early acts.
- Originality should come from how the seed develops, not from replacing the seed.
- Do not copy names, objects, or hooks from unrelated scenarios unless they are explicitly present in the seed or atoms.

STRUCTURE RULES
- Generate exactly 4 acts.
- Acts 1-3 must each contain 2-4 scenes.
- Act 4 must contain 1-3 scenes.
- Total scene count must be between 8 and 12.
- The scenario must not be solvable in a single clue chain or one uninterrupted push forward.
- Each act must have a distinct investigative purpose:
  - Act 1: establish the ordinary surface situation, the first anomaly, and at least one competing explanation.
  - Act 2: deepen the investigation through discovery, social conflict, obstruction, or danger.
  - Act 3: introduce a reversal, betrayal, contradiction, false conclusion, or recontextualization that changes the investigators' understanding.
  - Act 4: deliver the climax, hard choice, or final confrontation with more than one possible solution, tradeoff, or cost.
- Do not make the final solution a single object destruction unless that action has been heavily set up, carries a cost, and is not the only viable approach.
- Do not allow the scenario to move from hook to culprit to final confrontation in a straight line.

TWIST AND REVERSAL RULES
- Include at least:
  - 2 misleading surface explanations or false leads
  - 2 midpoint revelations that recontextualize earlier clues
  - 1 final twist, betrayal, or hard choice in the climax
- The hidden threat must not be fully inferable from the inciting hook alone.
- At least one important NPC must be more complicated than they first appear.
- At least one clue must appear to support the wrong theory before later evidence overturns it.

INVESTIGATION DEPTH RULE
- Provide layered investigation, not a straight line.
- Include at least 6 clues total.
- Include at least 2 clues that initially point to the wrong explanation.
- Include at least 2 clues that contradict an earlier assumption.
- Include at least 2 plot threads that can be pursued in parallel.
- Investigators should need to compare clues, not merely follow a single breadcrumb path.

SCENARIO BACKBONE RULE
- Before writing acts and scenes, internally design a dramatic backbone with:
  - a surface explanation
  - an actual explanation
  - false leads
  - contradictions
  - reversals
  - dynamic pressures
  - climax choices with costs
- Then express that backbone through the acts/scenes JSON below.
- The final JSON must include the scenario_engine object.

VALIDITY REQUIREMENTS
Your output is invalid unless all of the following are true:
- exactly 4 acts are present
- total scene count is between 8 and 12
- Acts 1-3 each contain at least 2 scenes
- at least 6 clues are present
- at least 2 twists are present
- at least 2 plot threads are present
- the climax offers more than one possible resolution, cost, or tradeoff
- the investigation includes at least one false lead and at least one contradiction
If any of these conditions are missing, revise the scenario before outputting the JSON.

SEED PRIORITY RULE
- The scenario seed is the primary source of truth for the hook, social setup, and central anomaly.
- Atoms may enrich, sharpen, or escalate the seed, but must not replace it.
