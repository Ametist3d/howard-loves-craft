CRITICAL SYNTHESIS RULES:
- Preserve the seed's central anomaly, social setup, and investigative premise.
- Use atoms as support material, not as replacement plots.
- Prefer coherence over novelty.
- You may combine elements from multiple atoms, but do NOT overwrite the seed with a stronger stock-horror template.

- Do not introduce a cult, ritual, portal, ancient god, or final-banishing spell unless the seed itself clearly points there.
- If an atom represents an endgame mechanic, do not let it dominate the hook or early acts.
- Originality should come from how the seed develops, not from replacing the seed.
- Do not copy names, objects, or hooks from unrelated scenarios unless they are explicitly present in the seed or atoms.

STRUCTURE RULES
- Generate exactly 4 acts.
- Acts 1-3 must each contain 2-4 scenes.
- Act 4 must contain 1-3 scenes.
- Total scene count must be between 8 and 12.
- The scenario must not be solvable in a single clue chain or one uninterrupted push forward.
- Each act must have a distinct investigative purpose:
  - Act 1: establish the ordinary surface situation, the first anomaly, and at least one competing explanation.
  - Act 2: deepen the investigation through discovery, social conflict, obstruction, or danger.
  - Act 3: introduce a reversal, betrayal, contradiction, false conclusion, or recontextualization that changes the investigators' understanding.
  - Act 4: deliver the climax, hard choice, or final confrontation with more than one possible solution, tradeoff, or cost.
- Do not make the final solution a single object destruction unless that action has been heavily set up, carries a cost, and is not the only viable approach.
- Do not allow the scenario to move from hook to culprit to final confrontation in a straight line.

TWIST AND REVERSAL RULES
- Include at least:
  - 2 misleading surface explanations or false leads
  - 2 midpoint revelations that recontextualize earlier clues
  - 1 final twist, betrayal, or hard choice in the climax
- The hidden threat must not be fully inferable from the inciting hook alone.
- At least one important NPC must be more complicated than they first appear.
- At least one clue must appear to support the wrong theory before later evidence overturns it.

INVESTIGATION DEPTH RULE
- Provide layered investigation, not a straight line.
- Include at least 6 clues total.
- Include at least 2 clues that initially point to the wrong explanation.
- Include at least 2 clues that contradict an earlier assumption.
- Include at least 2 plot threads that can be pursued in parallel.
- Investigators should need to compare clues, not merely follow a single breadcrumb path.

HOOK TYPE LIBRARY
HOOK TYPE PLANNER

Choose exactly 1 hook_type before writing the scenario. The hook defines the ordinary surface situation, the first actionable lead, the opening NPC posture, and the kind of misleading explanations that should appear early.

Allowed hook_type values:
- mystery_request
- investigation_request
- threat_warning
- unusual_event
- disappearance
- murder
- missing_shipment
- strange_signal
- escort_or_delivery
- recovery_job
- contamination_or_outbreak
- inheritance_or_document
- social_scandal
- official_coverup

Hook selection rules:
- Choose the hook_type that best preserves the seed's ordinary surface premise.
- Do not choose a lurid hook_type if the seed begins from a quiet or procedural situation.
- The hook must create a concrete first step that investigators can take immediately.
- The hook must not reveal the true hidden threat in plain form.
- The hook should imply at least 2 plausible wrong explanations.

Per-hook guidance:

mystery_request
- Someone asks investigators to explain a strange pattern, rumor, contradiction, or recurring anomaly.
- Early play should center on testimony, records, and scene comparison.
- Good false leads: superstition, fatigue, bad memory, gossip, local politics.

investigation_request
- A person, family, authority, employer, or institution hires or pressures investigators to look into a problem.
- Early play should create tension between official expectations and what is actually happening.
- Good false leads: bureaucratic failure, sabotage, petty crime, human corruption.

threat_warning
- Someone believes a danger is coming and asks for help before others take it seriously.
- Early play should balance credibility versus panic.
- Good false leads: paranoia, exaggeration, mistaken identity, routine hazard.

unusual_event
- A single disturbing, impossible, or ill-timed event disrupts an otherwise ordinary setting.
- Early play should stay close to the immediate aftermath and competing explanations.
- Good false leads: weather, equipment failure, prank, crowd confusion.

disappearance
- A person, group, vessel, item, or animal has gone missing under suspicious circumstances.
- Early play should emphasize timeline reconstruction and conflicting accounts.
- Good false leads: voluntary flight, accident, affair, theft, ordinary loss.

murder
- A death or apparent murder begins the case.
- Early play should preserve ambiguity around cause, motive, and opportunity.
- Good false leads: human jealousy, inheritance, revenge, drunken violence, local feud.

missing_shipment
- Something expected to arrive, depart, or be transferred has vanished or been diverted.
- Early play should foreground logistics, schedules, manifests, and institutional pressure.
- Good false leads: smuggling, clerical error, union conflict, piracy, weather.

strange_signal
- A message, signal, code, call, pattern, or transmission behaves as if it has intent or impossible origin.
- Early play should compare technical explanation against witness testimony.
- Good false leads: interference, fraud, military testing, atmospheric conditions.

escort_or_delivery
- Investigators must transport, escort, hand over, or protect something or someone.
- Early play should begin with the assignment and the first signs that the job's surface purpose is false.
- Good false leads: theft, interception, contractual betrayal, routine danger.

recovery_job
- Investigators are asked to retrieve an item, document, person, or device from a site.
- Early play should focus on why recovery matters and who else may want the target.
- Good false leads: salvage dispute, ownership conflict, trespass, academic rivalry.

contamination_or_outbreak
- Something is spreading, spoiling, infecting, or degrading bodies, minds, livestock, supply, or environment.
- Early play should not immediately confirm whether the cause is medical, industrial, environmental, or Mythos.
- Good false leads: toxin, disease, mold, food poisoning, industrial waste.

inheritance_or_document
- A will, map, diary, archive, legal packet, journal, or recovered paper draws investigators in.
- Early play should present the document as incomplete, misleading, or too easily dismissed.
- Good false leads: forgery, family lie, delusion, old scandal, bad translation.

social_scandal
- A rumor, affair, disgrace, or public embarrassment conceals the real disturbance.
- Early play should make reputation and secrecy matter as much as direct danger.
- Good false leads: blackmail, adultery, class resentment, political sabotage.

official_coverup
- Authorities, employers, clergy, police, port service, medical staff, or local elites suppress something.
- Early play should make the coverup plausible for human reasons before revealing what lies beneath it.
- Good false leads: corruption only, careerism, panic control, budget concealment.

Hook output requirement:
Before generating acts, internally determine:
- hook_type
- opening_surface_problem
- first_actionable_lead
- 2 plausible wrong explanations suggested by the hook

ACT MODULE TYPE LIBRARY
ACT MODULE PLANNER

Build the scenario from act modules, not from a fixed universal act formula. Choose 3-4 act modules total. 4 is preferred for full scenarios; 3 is allowed for compact scenarios; 5 is rare and only allowed if the seed clearly supports broader scope.

Each chosen act module must have:
- a distinct purpose
- a distinct mode of pressure
- a distinct kind of payoff
- a different relationship to the truth than the previous act

Allowed act_module_type values:
- arrival_and_briefing
- surface_inquiry
- witness_network
- site_investigation
- institutional_obstruction
- parallel_leads
- false_pattern_strengthens
- danger_probe
- hidden_logistics
- social_crack
- contradiction_reveal
- betrayal_or_complicity
- reversal_of_cause
- negotiated_window
- countdown_crisis
- chase_or_containment
- converging_threads
- costly_climax
- aftermath_choice

Act module definitions:

arrival_and_briefing
- Introduce investigators to the ordinary surface situation, tone, authority structure, and immediate first lead.
- Best for hooks that begin from a request, assignment, or arrival.

surface_inquiry
- Investigators talk to obvious people, inspect the obvious site, and gather explanations that seem to fit.
- Best for act 1 or early act 2.

witness_network
- The scenario deepens through multiple witnesses with incompatible stories.
- Good when social truth matters more than physical evidence.

site_investigation
- Physical search, scene comparison, records, architecture, or material anomalies deepen the case.
- Good when place and mechanism matter.

institutional_obstruction
- A human authority, employer, police force, port office, clinic, church, or owner blocks access.
- Use when social cost and secrecy are important.

parallel_leads
- At least 2 viable threads can be pursued in different order, and each changes interpretation of the other.
- Good mid-scenario module.

false_pattern_strengthens
- The wrong explanation becomes more convincing before being overturned.
- Use to prevent straight-line investigation.

danger_probe
- Investigators push into a risky space and gain evidence under immediate physical or psychological threat.
- Use when risk must replace passive clue collection.

hidden_logistics
- The mystery turns out to involve repeated practical operations: transport, storage, feeding, timing, labor, maintenance, signaling, routing, paperwork, access, ritual sequence, or supply movement.
- Good for scenarios where the Mythos behaves like a system rather than a single jump scare.

social_crack
- An NPC, witness, or authority reveals pressure, fear, contradiction, or personal complicity.
- Good bridge from act 1 into act 2 or act 3.

contradiction_reveal
- An earlier explanation is plainly contradicted by new evidence.
- This should materially change investigator assumptions.

betrayal_or_complicity
- Someone important is not what they seemed: not necessarily evil, but hiding, enabling, or rationalizing the problem.
- Good act 3 module.

reversal_of_cause
- Investigators learn that what they thought was the cause is actually the effect, the signal is actually a response, the victim is actually a trigger, the coverup was a panic response, etc.
- Use when the scenario's best twist is conceptual rather than personal.

negotiated_window
- A direct non-final contact or temporary understanding becomes possible.
- Good when climax should be more than simple attack/destroy.

countdown_crisis
- A deadline now dominates play: ship arrival, public ceremony, shift change, storm, tide, dawn, outbreak stage, execution, ritual threshold, convoy, inspection, election, broadcast.
- Use to force resolution pressure.

chase_or_containment
- Investigators must pursue, trap, isolate, or prevent transfer/escape rather than only discover more.
- Good late act module.

converging_threads
- Separate leads finally point to the same hidden structure.
- Best used late, before the climax.

costly_climax
- The scenario demands a real choice with loss, compromise, collateral harm, secrecy, exposure, bargain, or sacrifice.
- This should not devolve into one more clue chain.

afternath_choice
- Optional final module for outcomes, moral burden, concealment, fallout, arrest, cover story, or future threat.
- Use sparingly.

Composition rules:
- Do not repeat the same act_module_type twice unless the seed clearly justifies it.
- At least 1 chosen module must strengthen a wrong explanation before overturning it.
- At least 1 chosen module must create social or institutional pressure, not just physical exploration.
- At least 1 chosen module must materially recontextualize earlier clues.
- The final chosen module must be one of: costly_climax, countdown_crisis, chase_or_containment, negotiated_window.
- Once the core structure is already understood, later modules must stop adding tiny partial clues and instead produce a decision, confrontation, consequence, or transition.

Output planning requirement:
Before generating scenes, internally choose:
- act_count
- ordered list of act_module_type values
- what each module contributes to belief change

RESOLUTION TYPE LIBRARY
RESOLUTION TYPE PLANNER

Choose 2-4 possible resolution types for the climax. At least 2 must be viable. Each must carry a real cost, tradeoff, compromise, or future consequence. Do not make one obviously correct solution unless the seed explicitly demands tragedy.

Allowed resolution_type values:
- suppress
- expose
- contain
- bargain
- evacuate
- misdirect
- sever
- sacrifice
- transfer_cost
- delay
- destroy_with_cost
- human_authority_solution
- occult_or_technical_override
- escape_and_bury

Resolution definitions:

suppress
- Stop the immediate process, signal, ritual, transmission, exchange, or manifestation.
- Cost should affect safety, reputation, evidence, innocents, or future risk.

expose
- Reveal the truth publicly or to authority.
- Cost should be panic, disbelief, scapegoating, legal danger, loss of secrecy, or collapse of trust.

contain
- Keep the problem isolated without solving its deeper cause.
- Cost should be maintenance burden, secrecy, collateral damage, or future recurrence.

bargain
- Negotiate with the force, faction, or agency behind the problem.
- Cost should be moral compromise, future debt, hidden concession, or release of something unsafe.

evacuate
- Save lives by abandoning property, evidence, reputation, territory, or strategic advantage.
- Cost should be unresolved threat or loss of truth.

misdirect
- Feed false information, false navigation, false timing, false identity, or false target to redirect danger.
- Cost should be collateral harm, confusion, or loss of control.

sever
- Break the connection between two systems, groups, spaces, or signals.
- Cost should be damage to ordinary human functions, isolation, or irreversible loss.

sacrifice
- Someone, something, access, innocence, status, career, artifact, or place must be knowingly given up.
- Cost must be explicit, not symbolic only.

transfer_cost
- Save the current site or people by moving the burden elsewhere.
- Cost should make the moral compromise visible.

delay
- Prevent disaster now, without truly ending it.
- Cost should be that the scenario leaves a future wound or looming return.

destroy_with_cost
- Destroy a key object, structure, or process, but only if it has real setup and real collateral cost.
- This must never be the sole obvious resolution.

human_authority_solution
- Use law, force, quarantine, port power, church authority, military pressure, institutional shutdown, or bureaucracy.
- Cost should be coverup, violence, innocent harm, or loss of truth.

occult_or_technical_override
- Use understanding of the pattern, machinery, code, rite, architecture, or procedure to alter the outcome.
- Cost should be risk, irreversibility, or making an already bad system temporarily functional.

escape_and_bury
- Survive and conceal the truth rather than resolve it cleanly.
- Cost should be guilt, future recurrence, and damaged credibility.

Resolution rules:
- At least 1 resolution must protect immediate human life.
- At least 1 resolution must impose a moral or political cost.
- At least 1 resolution must create an uncertain future consequence.
- If destroy_with_cost is present, it must not be the only viable option.
- If bargain is present, it must not guarantee safety or honesty.
- If expose is present, it must not promise that people will believe the truth.

Climax behavior rule:
- When the investigation has already exposed the core structure of the problem, the scenario must transition into a resolution state.
- In that state, do not continue feeding more tiny clues about the same mechanism, site, or truth.
- Instead, force one of the following:
  - confrontation
  - negotiation
  - costly decision
  - emergency rerouting
  - containment action
  - sacrifice or escape

Output planning requirement:
Before generating act 4, internally choose:
- 2-4 resolution_type values
- immediate cost of each
- longer consequence of each
