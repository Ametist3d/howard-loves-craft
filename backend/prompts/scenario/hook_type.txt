HOOK TYPE PLANNER

Choose exactly 1 hook_type before writing the scenario. The hook defines the ordinary surface situation, the first actionable lead, the opening NPC posture, and the kind of misleading explanations that should appear early.

Allowed hook_type values:
- mystery_request
- investigation_request
- threat_warning
- unusual_event
- disappearance
- murder
- missing_shipment
- strange_signal
- escort_or_delivery
- recovery_job
- contamination_or_outbreak
- inheritance_or_document
- social_scandal
- official_coverup

Hook selection rules:
- Choose the hook_type that best preserves the seed's ordinary surface premise.
- Do not choose a lurid hook_type if the seed begins from a quiet or procedural situation.
- The hook must create a concrete first step that investigators can take immediately.
- The hook must not reveal the true hidden threat in plain form.
- The hook should imply at least 2 plausible wrong explanations.

Per-hook guidance:

mystery_request
- Someone asks investigators to explain a strange pattern, rumor, contradiction, or recurring anomaly.
- Early play should center on testimony, records, and scene comparison.
- Good false leads: superstition, fatigue, bad memory, gossip, local politics.

investigation_request
- A person, family, authority, employer, or institution hires or pressures investigators to look into a problem.
- Early play should create tension between official expectations and what is actually happening.
- Good false leads: bureaucratic failure, sabotage, petty crime, human corruption.

threat_warning
- Someone believes a danger is coming and asks for help before others take it seriously.
- Early play should balance credibility versus panic.
- Good false leads: paranoia, exaggeration, mistaken identity, routine hazard.

unusual_event
- A single disturbing, impossible, or ill-timed event disrupts an otherwise ordinary setting.
- Early play should stay close to the immediate aftermath and competing explanations.
- Good false leads: weather, equipment failure, prank, crowd confusion.

disappearance
- A person, group, vessel, item, or animal has gone missing under suspicious circumstances.
- Early play should emphasize timeline reconstruction and conflicting accounts.
- Good false leads: voluntary flight, accident, affair, theft, ordinary loss.

murder
- A death or apparent murder begins the case.
- Early play should preserve ambiguity around cause, motive, and opportunity.
- Good false leads: human jealousy, inheritance, revenge, drunken violence, local feud.

missing_shipment
- Something expected to arrive, depart, or be transferred has vanished or been diverted.
- Early play should foreground logistics, schedules, manifests, and institutional pressure.
- Good false leads: smuggling, clerical error, union conflict, piracy, weather.

strange_signal
- A message, signal, code, call, pattern, or transmission behaves as if it has intent or impossible origin.
- Early play should compare technical explanation against witness testimony.
- Good false leads: interference, fraud, military testing, atmospheric conditions.

escort_or_delivery
- Investigators must transport, escort, hand over, or protect something or someone.
- Early play should begin with the assignment and the first signs that the job's surface purpose is false.
- Good false leads: theft, interception, contractual betrayal, routine danger.

recovery_job
- Investigators are asked to retrieve an item, document, person, or device from a site.
- Early play should focus on why recovery matters and who else may want the target.
- Good false leads: salvage dispute, ownership conflict, trespass, academic rivalry.

contamination_or_outbreak
- Something is spreading, spoiling, infecting, or degrading bodies, minds, livestock, supply, or environment.
- Early play should not immediately confirm whether the cause is medical, industrial, environmental, or Mythos.
- Good false leads: toxin, disease, mold, food poisoning, industrial waste.

inheritance_or_document
- A will, map, diary, archive, legal packet, journal, or recovered paper draws investigators in.
- Early play should present the document as incomplete, misleading, or too easily dismissed.
- Good false leads: forgery, family lie, delusion, old scandal, bad translation.

social_scandal
- A rumor, affair, disgrace, or public embarrassment conceals the real disturbance.
- Early play should make reputation and secrecy matter as much as direct danger.
- Good false leads: blackmail, adultery, class resentment, political sabotage.

official_coverup
- Authorities, employers, clergy, police, port service, medical staff, or local elites suppress something.
- Early play should make the coverup plausible for human reasons before revealing what lies beneath it.
- Good false leads: corruption only, careerism, panic control, budget concealment.

Hook output requirement:
Before generating acts, internally determine:
- hook_type
- opening_surface_problem
- first_actionable_lead
- 2 plausible wrong explanations suggested by the hook
