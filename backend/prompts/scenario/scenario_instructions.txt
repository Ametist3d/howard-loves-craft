ERA/SETTING CONSTRAINT: {era_context}
Strictly enforce this setting. No 1920s tropes unless the era explicitly states them.

NAMING CONVENTION:
- All proper nouns (people, places, artifacts, institutions) must fit the setting, era, and social context.
- Do not reuse unrelated names, hook objects, or iconic details from prior scenarios unless strongly justified by the seed or atoms.

BANNED CLICHÉS:
- impossible geometry / impossible angles
- non-Euclidean
- ozone as a default atmospheric descriptor
- eldritch used more than once in the whole output
- cyclopean
- gibbous / blasphemous geometry
- organic patterns on surfaces
- pulsating as a generic horror adjective

CORE SYNTHESIS RULES:
- Preserve the seed's central anomaly, social setup, and investigative premise.
- Use atoms as support material, not as replacement plots.
- Prefer coherence over novelty.
- Keep the early scenario close to the seed's ordinary surface situation.
- Do not introduce cults, portals, banishment rituals, or ancient gods unless the seed clearly supports them.
- If an atom represents an endgame mechanic, do not let it dominate the hook or early acts.
- Originality should come from how the seed develops, not from replacing the seed.
- Do not copy names, objects, or hooks from unrelated scenarios unless they are explicitly present in the seed or atoms.

STRUCTURE RULES:
- Respect the externally supplied structural budget.
- target_act_count is authoritative.
- target_total_scenes is authoritative.
- scene_count_plan is authoritative.
- The scenario must not be solvable in a single clue chain or one uninterrupted push forward.
- Each act must have a distinct investigative purpose.
- The final act must deliver a climax, hard choice, costly compromise, confrontation, containment, negotiation, or aftermath.
- Do not make the final solution a single object destruction unless it has been set up, carries a cost, and is not the only viable option.
- Do not allow the scenario to move from hook to culprit to final confrontation in a straight line.

TWIST AND REVERSAL RULES:
- Include at least 2 misleading surface explanations or false leads.
- Include at least 2 contradictions or midpoint revelations that recontextualize earlier clues.
- Include at least 1 final twist, betrayal, or hard choice in the climax.
- The hidden threat must not be fully inferable from the inciting hook alone.
- At least one important NPC must be more complicated than they first appear.
- At least one clue should appear to support the wrong theory before later evidence overturns it.

INVESTIGATION DEPTH RULE:
- Provide layered investigation, not a straight line.
- Include parallel leads when possible.
- Investigators should need to compare clues, not merely follow one breadcrumb path.
- Social pressure, institutional friction, procedural obstruction, or danger should matter.
- Once the core structure is understood, stop dripping tiny clues and move toward decision, confrontation, consequence, or escape.

SCENARIO BACKBONE RULE:
- Before writing final scenes, internally design a dramatic backbone with:
  - a surface explanation
  - an actual explanation
  - false leads
  - contradictions
  - reversals
  - dynamic pressures
  - climax choices with costs
- Then express that backbone through the act and scene outputs.
- The final blueprint must include the scenario_engine object.

REGISTRY RULES:
- acts.scenes are the primary scenario structure.
- locations, npcs, clues, and plot_threads are compact canonical registries.
- Keep registries short and non-redundant.
- Do NOT repeat full scene prose inside registries.
- Prefer one short sentence or compact fragment per field.
- Keep only information that is not already obvious from the scene text.
- Do NOT add extra sections beyond the schema required by the stage.

REGISTRY FIELD RULES:
- locations:
  - each item must contain only: name, tags, hidden
  - keep tags compact
  - hidden should be the one most important concealed fact about the place
- npcs:
  - each item must contain only: name, role, secret, motivation
  - role must be one of: ally|neutral|enemy|hidden_enemy
- clues:
  - each item must contain only: title, content, true_meaning, location
  - content = surface meaning
  - true_meaning = deeper meaning
- plot_threads:
  - each item must contain only: name, stakes, steps
  - keep steps as a small integer, usually 3-5

LENGTH DISCIPLINE:
- Keep prose concise.
- Put most narrative richness into acts and scenes, not registries.
- Avoid repetition between scene descriptions, clue text, and registry entries.
- Short functional prose is better than ornate bloat.
