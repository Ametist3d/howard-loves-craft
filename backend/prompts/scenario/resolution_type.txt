RESOLUTION TYPE PLANNER

Choose 2-4 possible resolution types for the climax. At least 2 must be viable. Each must carry a real cost, tradeoff, compromise, or future consequence. Do not make one obviously correct solution unless the seed explicitly demands tragedy.

Allowed resolution_type values:
- suppress
- expose
- contain
- bargain
- evacuate
- misdirect
- sever
- sacrifice
- transfer_cost
- delay
- destroy_with_cost
- human_authority_solution
- occult_or_technical_override
- escape_and_bury

Resolution definitions:

suppress
- Stop the immediate process, signal, ritual, transmission, exchange, or manifestation.
- Cost should affect safety, reputation, evidence, innocents, or future risk.

expose
- Reveal the truth publicly or to authority.
- Cost should be panic, disbelief, scapegoating, legal danger, loss of secrecy, or collapse of trust.

contain
- Keep the problem isolated without solving its deeper cause.
- Cost should be maintenance burden, secrecy, collateral damage, or future recurrence.

bargain
- Negotiate with the force, faction, or agency behind the problem.
- Cost should be moral compromise, future debt, hidden concession, or release of something unsafe.

evacuate
- Save lives by abandoning property, evidence, reputation, territory, or strategic advantage.
- Cost should be unresolved threat or loss of truth.

misdirect
- Feed false information, false navigation, false timing, false identity, or false target to redirect danger.
- Cost should be collateral harm, confusion, or loss of control.

sever
- Break the connection between two systems, groups, spaces, or signals.
- Cost should be damage to ordinary human functions, isolation, or irreversible loss.

sacrifice
- Someone, something, access, innocence, status, career, artifact, or place must be knowingly given up.
- Cost must be explicit, not symbolic only.

transfer_cost
- Save the current site or people by moving the burden elsewhere.
- Cost should make the moral compromise visible.

delay
- Prevent disaster now, without truly ending it.
- Cost should be that the scenario leaves a future wound or looming return.

destroy_with_cost
- Destroy a key object, structure, or process, but only if it has real setup and real collateral cost.
- This must never be the sole obvious resolution.

human_authority_solution
- Use law, force, quarantine, port power, church authority, military pressure, institutional shutdown, or bureaucracy.
- Cost should be coverup, violence, innocent harm, or loss of truth.

occult_or_technical_override
- Use understanding of the pattern, machinery, code, rite, architecture, or procedure to alter the outcome.
- Cost should be risk, irreversibility, or making an already bad system temporarily functional.

escape_and_bury
- Survive and conceal the truth rather than resolve it cleanly.
- Cost should be guilt, future recurrence, and damaged credibility.

Resolution rules:
- At least 1 resolution must protect immediate human life.
- At least 1 resolution must impose a moral or political cost.
- At least 1 resolution must create an uncertain future consequence.
- If destroy_with_cost is present, it must not be the only viable option.
- If bargain is present, it must not guarantee safety or honesty.
- If expose is present, it must not promise that people will believe the truth.

Climax behavior rule:
- When the investigation has already exposed the core structure of the problem, the scenario must transition into a resolution state.
- In that state, do not continue feeding more tiny clues about the same mechanism, site, or truth.
- Instead, force one of the following:
  - confrontation
  - negotiation
  - costly decision
  - emergency rerouting
  - containment action
  - sacrifice or escape

Output planning requirement:
Before generating act 4, internally choose:
- 2-4 resolution_type values
- immediate cost of each
- longer consequence of each
