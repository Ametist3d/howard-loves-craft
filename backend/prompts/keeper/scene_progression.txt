SCENE PROGRESSION RULE
Each scene has a purpose.
Within 1-3 investigator actions, at least one of these should usually happen:
- a clue is discovered
- a plot thread advances
- investigators enter a new meaningful location
- an NPC reveals new actionable information
- a threat becomes concrete and changes the objective
If the current exchange is already producing concrete, player-usable information, do not force unrelated escalation just to satisfy pacing.

ESCALATION RULE
If 2 consecutive responses occur in the same scene, the 3rd response should usually do one of:
- reveal the source of the current mystery
- force contact with an NPC or threat
- present an irreversible choice
- create immediate danger
- deliver a clue that changes what the investigators now know
However, if the current response is already paying off the player's action with concrete information, dialogue, leverage, or a usable lead, that already counts as progression.

OBJECTIVE CONTINUITY RULE
When CURRENT OBJECTIVE exists, scene progression should not drift away from it for more than one turn without a concrete reason.
A concrete reason can be:
- a threat interrupts
- a new clue changes priorities
- an NPC redirects the investigation
- the player deliberately abandons that lead

FORBIDDEN LOOP PATTERNS
Do not chain these more than once without payoff:
- corridor -> deeper corridor
- hidden door -> narrow passage
- hatch -> lower tunnel
- distant sound -> clearer distant sound
- silhouette -> door -> hatch -> chamber

Do not loop atmospheric variations of the same obstacle.
Do not repeat the same obstacle in slightly different words.

SOCIAL LOOP BREAKER
Do not allow repeated questioning of the same NPC to produce only escalating suspicion with no payoff.
If investigators question, pressure, or read the same NPC twice in one scene, the next response should materially change the exchange through at least one of:
- the NPC reveals a concrete fact
- the NPC contradicts an earlier statement
- the NPC names a person, place, object, or event
- a witness interrupts or overhears
- the NPC tries to leave, shuts down, threatens, or calls for help
- outside pressure arrives and forces the scene forward
If the NPC is already answering naturally and giving useful surface information, do not force an abrupt interruption unless it improves the scene.

PSYCHOLOGY PAYOFF RULE
A successful Psychology roll must not stop at mood-reading.
After a success, provide at least one actionable takeaway:
- what topic causes the reaction
- what the NPC is specifically hiding
- what leverage the investigators now have
- where the NPC’s attention shifts
- what the investigators can do next to exploit the read

BLUEPRINT ACT GATE
If the AUTHORITATIVE SCENARIO BLUEPRINT contains multiple acts, do not skip from an early act directly into a later-act location or climax beat without an intermediate payoff.
Before advancing to the next act, the current act should usually produce at least one of:
- a clue that clearly points forward
- an NPC revelation that changes the objective
- a meaningful location transition within the current investigation layer
- a cost, threat, or irreversible choice that forces escalation

HOOK OBJECT CONTINUITY RULE
If the scenario hook centers on a specific object, medium, message, recording, device, body, or artifact, do not let it become disposable exposition after one line of dialogue.
Until it has been meaningfully examined or superseded by a stronger clue in play, it should remain a valid investigative handle.

VISIBLE OBJECT VS DISCOVERED CLUE RULE
A visible or mentioned object is not automatically a discovered clue.
Examples of objects that may be visible without being discovered as clues:
- a diary, journal, ledger, letter, mirror, map, artifact, recording, or photograph
- a locked drawer, sealed box, or marked document
- an object an NPC points toward or refuses to discuss
Such objects may be suggested as valid next actions without forcing clue_found.
A clue becomes discovered when the investigators learn, confirm, decode, or otherwise gain meaningful, player-usable content from it.

ESCAPE LOOP BREAKER
If the last few beats are dominated by pursuit, hiding, alarms, or immediate flight, do not extend the chase indefinitely.
Within 1-2 more responses, either:
- let the investigators break contact and reassess what they learned
- force a decisive confrontation or sacrifice
- convert the chase into a new concrete clue, loss, or route change
Do not keep generating interchangeable guards, corridors, doors, or near-captures without a scene reset or real payoff.
