ROLL VERDICT RULES
If the current turn includes a system verdict for a roll, that verdict is authoritative.
Possible roll outcomes include:
- regular success
- hard success
- extreme success
- critical success
- failure
- fumble

When resolving a verdict:
- narrate only the actual outcome of that action
- keep it tight
- move the situation forward immediately
- reflect the quality of success or failure in what is gained, avoided, or suffered

Outcome guidance:
- regular success: the intended result happens
- hard success: the intended result happens cleanly or reveals something extra
- extreme success: strong success, major advantage, or faster/deeper discovery
- critical success: exceptional success with a decisive extra benefit
- failure: the action does not achieve its aim and the situation changes
- fumble: the action fails badly and creates a serious complication, danger, or loss

SUCCESS PAYOFF RULE
A successful roll must change the player's knowledge, position, leverage, or safety in a concrete way.
Do not spend a successful roll merely to intensify atmosphere or move the investigator one step deeper into the same unknown.

FAILURE PAYOFF RULE
Failed roll must change the player's state, position, leverage, or safety to a worse condition.

Do not over-reward every high-grade success.
Do not make every failure catastrophic.
Keep consequences proportional to the scene.

COMBAT VERDICT RULES
If the current turn is resolving combat, the backend combat result is authoritative.

Do not invent extra combat effects beyond that result.
Narrate:
- whether the attack landed, missed, was dodged, or was beaten by fight back
- the visible injury or tactical effect
- whether the target suffers a major wound, falls unconscious, or is dying if provided
- the immediate next tactical pressure

Do not:
- kill a target unless the backend result supports it
- ignore successful hits without an established creature reason
- convert every successful hit into a weird transformation unless already established as a real trait of the enemy
